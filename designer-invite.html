The Shift02
Where this is going

Gardens are about to be measured.
The only open question is on whose terms.

Councils are buying biodiversity data. Buyers are starting to ask what a garden is worth ecologically, not just aesthetically. A number is coming for our work whether we author it or not.

We would rather the people who actually understand Country — designers who plant to place, who read an EVC before they read a mood board — be the ones who define what that number means. Not a consultancy that has never knelt in the soil.

What It Is03
Not a portfolio — a standard

A living register of ecological gardens,
scored on one transparent methodology.

Every garden in the Registry is scored 0–100 across five pillars — Biodiversity, Soil & Water, Habitat, Connectivity, and Evidence. The method is open, EVC-aware, and built to align with serious environmental-accounting frameworks. A score isn't a sticker. It's a position you can defend to a client, a council, or a buyer.

Why Now04
A standard gets written once

You can be a subject of it,
or a co-author of it.

Co-author · early

Help define the method while it's still soft

Your gardens, your judgement, and your read of Country shape what "good" means on the scale. Early designers are credited as founders of the standard, not measured against someone else's.

Subject · later

Inherit a number someone else decided

Wait, and the methodology hardens without you. You score your work against criteria written by people who weren't in the field — and explain to clients why their garden lost points to a rubric you had no hand in.

The Network Effect06
Why neighbours matter

Your gardens are worth more with others nearby.

The methodology rewards connectivity. A garden that sits within a corridor of other ecological gardens scores higher than the same garden in isolation — because that's how Country actually works. Habitat needs continuity.

So enrolling alongside other thoughtful designers isn't charity to a competitor. It's how each of our gardens becomes a corridor node instead of an island — and how the whole network lifts every score inside it.
