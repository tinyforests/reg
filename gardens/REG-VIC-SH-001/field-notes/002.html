↓ About

A small stay held within a larger landscape.

This is not a resort stay. It is a quieter kind of accommodation — one shaped by restoration, observation, and the slower rhythms of a living landscape.

Set within a nature reserve at Separation Creek, the stay offers a rare experience of being immersed in coastal forest while remaining held by comfort, warmth, and thoughtful design.

The atmosphere is simple, intimate, and grounded. Wildlife moves through the site. Light shifts across the trees. Ocean air arrives softly through the valley.

It is a place for resting, recalibrating, reading, walking, and remembering what it feels like to be close to the natural world.

↓ Caravan

A lovingly restored 1940s bondwood caravan.

At the centre of the stay is a beautifully restored bondwood caravan dating from the 1940s. Its original character has been retained wherever possible, allowing the material warmth and atmosphere of the structure to remain intact.

The result is accommodation that feels both nostalgic and refined — modest in footprint, but rich in texture, story, and sensory experience.

Heritage presence, carefully reimagined for quiet modern retreat.

↓ Immersion

Forest bathing, wildlife, and coastal calm.

The experience here is shaped less by programmed activity and more by atmosphere. The reserve invites quiet immersion — stepping outside early, listening for birds, watching changing weather move through the trees, and settling into the pace of the site.

Forest bathing

A place to slow down and return to the body through landscape, air, scent, and stillness.

Wildlife presence

Birdlife, insects, and native animals remain part of the daily experience of the site.

Scenic coastal setting

The stay sits within the broader beauty of the Otway coast, with the feeling of sea and forest held together.
