Field Note #005

Arundel • Surrey Hills, VIC
Swales Water habitat Infiltration Amphibians

Excavation for swales and water habitat

Swales and/or shallow basins excavated and formed to slow runoff, increase infiltration, and create seasonal water habitat potential within the garden matrix.

Draft
Date: TBD
Observer: Gardener & Son
Design principle
Seasonal water, not permanent pond
Risk control
No mosquito traps
Shallow, drains / infiltrates
Habitat intent
Moisture refuge + breeding potential

Observation

Describe the swales: length, depth, position on contour, overflow direction, and where water enters and exits.

Why this matters

  • • Swales convert episodic runoff into stored soil moisture.
  • • Seasonal wetting supports fungi, invertebrates, and amphibian refuge.
  • • Habitat diversity increases when wet/dry cycles are designed, not accidental.

Verification plan

  • • Photograph during a rain event, including inflow, pooling, and overflow.
  • • Record infiltration time in hours until the basin drains.
  • • Note any amphibian activity after rainfall.
