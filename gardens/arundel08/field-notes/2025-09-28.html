Site documentation -- pre-installation baseline

Comprehensive site documentation completed two months before installation commenced. The visit records the full pre-installation condition of the rear garden -- slope, existing vegetation, materials, infrastructure, and park adjacency. These photographs form the visual baseline against which all ecological change will be measured. The site shows a steeply sloped rear garden with mixed exotic and native vegetation, existing brick retaining walls and stone steps, a rainwater harvesting system already installed, and direct adjacency to Surrey Hills Reserve through the rear fence.

Surrey Hills Reserve -- the garden's ecological neighbour

The intact remnant vegetation of Surrey Hills Reserve sits directly beyond the rear fence. Site observations

  • -- The rear garden is a steeply sloped site with strong solar access on the upper slope and a moisture-retaining lower zone -- ideal conditions for the five-layer ecological planting strategy.
  • -- Existing brick retaining walls and stone steps provide structural material that will be integrated into the new design rather than removed -- reducing waste and maintaining material character.
  • -- Weed pressure is high across the slope, dominated by exotic grasses and sorrel. Removal will be a priority before installation to give the indigenous planting the best possible establishment conditions.
  • -- Rainwater harvesting system is already installed on the side of the house -- a significant infrastructure asset that will supply irrigation once the planting is established.
  • -- The rear fence boundary directly adjoins Surrey Hills Reserve. The park canopy is visible in multiple photographs -- the corridor connection is immediate and unambiguous.
  • -- Existing stone steps and brick walls show the site's long habitation -- these structures become part of the ecological story rather than obstacles to it.
  • -- Plants have been staged and are ready for installation -- the design is committed and sourcing is complete.
