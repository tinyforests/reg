Excavation, soil removal and weeding

Site preparation commenced in earnest. Unwanted and contaminated soil excavated and removed from the slope. Existing plants removed across the planting zones. Weeding completed. The slope is now cleared to bare soil, ready for contouring, amendment and planting. Three photographs document the progression from partially cleared slope through to the bare excavated face -- the site's first transformation from its pre-installation condition.

Why this matters

  • -- Removal of contaminated and compacted soil from the slope creates clean planting conditions -- essential for the deep-rooting species in the design palette, particularly the canopy and sub-canopy layer.
  • -- Weeding before planting eliminates the weed seed bank at the soil surface, giving indigenous species the best possible establishment advantage without chemical intervention.
  • -- The bare slope is not a degraded state -- it is a prepared canvas. The absence of vegetation is temporary and deliberate. Within weeks of planting, the ground layer will begin closing the surface.
  • -- The brick retaining wall exposed through clearing will be integrated into the final design as a structural feature, softened by planting above and below.
