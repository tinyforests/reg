On machinery and ecological intent

The use of a mini loader is a pragmatic decision, not an ecological compromise. The Kanga's compact footprint and low ground pressure reduce compaction relative to larger machinery, and the speed it provides allows the soil surface to be cleared, levelled and rested before the planting phase rather than worked repeatedly by hand over a longer period. The wet conditions visible in these photographs -- the mud, the tyre tracks, the saturated soil -- will recover. Within weeks of mulching and planting, the surface will begin to knit together. What matters is what comes next.
