Aerial view from deck -- full slope cleared

The first clear overview of the entire 475m2 rear garden in its cleared state. The Reserve canopy is vivid in full spring flush beyond the fence. Stacked bricks, irrigation coil and hose, scattered debris -- the site mid-process. The work that happens now determines the ecological performance of the next 30 years.

The goddess wears earmuffs

A stone garden figure fitted with hearing protection against the noise of the works. The Reserve eucalypts are in full spring flush behind her. She has been on this site longer than most of the plants. She will stay.

Cleared slope -- wheelbarrow, statue, Reserve canopy

The scale of the cleared slope visible from ground level. Red marker flags in soil indicate planting positions. Reserve canopy spring green against blue sky. The goddess watching from the left, earmuffs on.

Spring in Surrey Hills Reserve

The Reserve canopy visible in all three photographs is in full spring flush -- the bright green of new growth against the dry grey of bare branches. This is the ecological condition the design is working toward. Within two years of planting, the garden's own canopy will begin to mirror this energy. The distance between the cleared soil and the canopy beyond the fence is measured in weeks of planting and years of growth -- not in kind.
