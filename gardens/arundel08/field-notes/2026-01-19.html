Sandy path corridor with loader tracks showing scale and direction of works. Fountain urn retained right -- it will stay. Existing planted border right edge. Blue tarp protecting retained materials. Fence line and full canopy of Reserve and neighbouring trees beyond -- the ecological context that gives the whole project meaning.

1 -- Mechanical weed removal

Mini loader used to scrape and remove remaining surface weed species across the site. Works targeted shallow-rooted weeds and accumulated organic debris. Removal undertaken selectively to avoid unnecessary disturbance of retained soil and emerging planting zones.

2 -- Pathway alignment and rough levelling

Future path corridors clarified and lightly scraped. Existing informal movement lines reinforced rather than erased. Ground levels adjusted to reduce high points and soften transitions without creating hard edges or flat planes.

3 -- Early landform shaping

Initial contour shaping undertaken to guide future circulation and water movement. Soil feathered and blended to maintain a naturalistic reading of the land. Particular focus on improving flow between access points and key garden zones.

4 -- Surface preparation

Loose material consolidated where possible. Site left in a condition suitable for sub-base installation in subsequent stages. Further fine hand-weeding required in areas inaccessible to machinery.

Methodology -- intentional and ecological

Mechanical works were intentionally shallow and controlled. Priority given throughout to preserving soil biology, avoiding compaction and hard pan formation, and maintaining existing site character and slope relationships. The loader was a tool of refinement, not transformation.
