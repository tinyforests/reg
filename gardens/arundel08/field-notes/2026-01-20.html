Design decision -- granite and gabions

The granite rocks present on site have been intentionally allocated to gabion structures rather than used as feature rocks. This decision reflects a considered material hierarchy: gabions allow the granite to contribute structurally without dominating the visual language of the garden. It preserves on-site stone through reuse while ensuring visual coherence. The decision creates space for the later introduction of a more naturalistic, softer-edged feature rock that better supports the ecological and landscape aesthetic of the final design.
