Why this matters

  • -- Ground contouring before planting creates varied moisture and temperature conditions, allowing different species to find their preferred microclimate within the same bed.
  • -- Manual preparation preserves existing soil structure and fungal networks rather than disrupting them with machinery.
  • -- Passive water direction reduces irrigation dependency from establishment onward and supports seasonal moisture buffering.
  • -- Micro-relief creates physical complexity at soil level -- important for insects, skinks, and ground-foraging birds from day one.
