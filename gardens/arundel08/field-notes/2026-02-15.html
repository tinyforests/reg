Habitat structure installed -- embedded logs and voids

Habitat structure installation completed across the lower slope and mid-canopy zone. Logs selected for advanced decay state were partially embedded into the soil profile at varying angles, creating void spaces beneath and within the wood. These structures provide immediate shelter, thermal regulation, moisture retention, and invertebrate habitat. Positioning was deliberate -- lower slope for moisture and amphibian use, mid-zone for lizards and ground-foraging birds.

Why this matters

  • -- Partially embedded logs bridge the soil and above-ground environment -- insects, fungi and small fauna can move between the two via the wood structure.
  • -- Advanced decay material supports mycorrhizal networks and soil microbiology as it breaks down, feeding the planting from below.
  • -- Void spaces beneath and within logs create stable microclimates -- cooler and moister than the surrounding soil, critical for refugia in summer.
  • -- Positioning on the lower slope anticipates burrowing frog and skink habitat -- both species were confirmed within days of installation.

Next steps

  • -- Fauna monitoring commencing -- log zones to be checked at each visit.
  • -- Planting around log structures to establish canopy over the habitat zone.
  • -- 2026-02-15to follow -- burrowing frog sighting confirmed three days after installation.
