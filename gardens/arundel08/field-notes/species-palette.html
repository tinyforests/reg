Design intent

  • -- The palette is structured around the Grassy Woodland EVC -- the pre-European ecological community of the Surrey Hills area. Each species is either indigenous to this community or native to south-eastern Victoria.
  • -- Two eucalypts (E. leucoxylon connata and E. radiata) form the eventual canopy, with Kunzea baxteri and Acacia verticillata as the subcanopy frame. These are long-horizon plants -- their full value will be realised over 10-20 years.
  • -- The groundcover and grass layer is designed to close over quickly, suppressing weeds and building soil biology from the base up. Viola hederacea, Dichondra repens and the grasses will knit the surface together within one growing season.
  • -- The corridor relationship with South Surrey Park is the ecological ambition. As the planting matures and canopy closes, this garden will function as a genuine stepping stone for fauna moving between the Reserve and the surrounding neighbourhood.
