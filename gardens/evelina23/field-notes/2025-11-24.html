Groundworks -- soil, mulch, rocks and habitat materials installed

Foundation day for Maggie's Garden. Bio mulch blend and premium organic soil laid across the 3x8m bed. Igneous rocks placed throughout to create habitat refuges and visual anchoring. Habitat logs embedded. Log steppers positioned to define movement through the space. Site blown down and tidied. The bed now wraps around the power pole and follows the driveway edge -- its purpose immediately legible even before a plant is in the ground.

Why this sequence matters

  • -- Soil and mulch installed before planting ensures every plant goes into prepared, amended ground rather than being planted and mulched around -- a cleaner result with less root disturbance.
  • -- Rocks and logs placed before planting allows their positions to inform plant placement -- habitat structure and planting work together rather than being retrofitted.
  • -- The power pole, normally an eyesore, becomes the garden's anchor. The bed embracing it is the design gesture that makes the whole thing read as intentional.
