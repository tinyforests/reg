Maggie's Garden -- alive from day one

  • -- The kangaroo paw flowering on planting day is unusual and auspicious. It tells you the plant was well-grown and well-handled -- it arrived with energy and expressed it immediately.
  • -- Mycorrhizal dip at planting establishes the fungal network from day one -- every root in contact with the inoculant begins building the underground infrastructure that will sustain the garden through dry periods without irrigation dependency.
  • -- The bird bath is not decorative. It is a water source for small birds and insects in a neighbourhood that offers very few. It will be used within days of planting.
  • -- High-density planting at 6-10 plants per m2 means the garden will close over quickly -- suppressing weeds, building microclimate, and giving neighbours and passers-by something remarkable to watch develop through the seasons.
