27 Apr 2026 Mont Albert North ER-VIC-MAN-EVE-001 Site Visit

Five months after planting, Maggie's Garden is in excellent progress. The wild native meadow is establishing well -- layered structure visible, soil health excellent, groundcovers spreading. A cutback of Einadia nutans and replacement of the dead scrambler with Hardenbergia violacea completed during the visit. Irrigation to be reduced.

Evelina -- 27 Apr 2026 -- Natasha assessing plant health, Einadia nutans in foreground
Site Observations
Soil Health

Soil health excellent -- deep mulch layer intact, moisture retained at depth. Active worm activity observed. Upgrade to 4/5.

Einadia nutans -- Cutback

Nodding Saltbush had extended aggressively into adjacent planting zones. Hard cutback performed to restore spacing and allow understorey species to establish. Will reshoot strongly.

Scrambler Replacement

Dead scrambler removed and replaced with Hardenbergia violacea (Happy Wanderer). Hardenbergia already present in the palette -- reinforces the fence line and provides year-round purple flower colour and pollinator value.

Overall Establishment

Garden is in excellent progress for five months post-planting. Layered structure visible -- groundcovers spreading, shrubs filling, grasses moving well. The wild meadow character is beginning to read clearly.
