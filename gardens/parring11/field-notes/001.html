Parring -- Balwyn, VIC
Groundworks Installation Habitat Soil

Installation begins -- soil, mulch, habitat rocks and micro-topology

Installation commenced today. Underperforming vegetation removed including camellia and buxus. New soil and mulch installed. Habitat rocks placed throughout the garden beds. Micro-topology created across planting zones to guide water movement and create varied conditions for plant establishment.

Why this matters

  • -- Removal of camellia and buxus reduces root competition and opens the soil surface to light and indigenous recruitment.
  • -- New soil and bio mulch improves water retention, microbial activity and planting establishment conditions.
  • -- Habitat rocks create thermal mass, shelter zones, and soil anchors that support lizards, insects and fungi from day one.
  • -- Micro-topology shapes water movement passively -- directing runoff into planting zones rather than across hard surfaces.

Next steps

  • -- Planting installation -- all 28 documented species to be installed across ground, shrub and emerging canopy layers.
  • -- Fauna monitoring to commence once planting is established.
  • -- Score reassessment at 6 months.
