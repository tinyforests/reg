Why this matters

  • -- Mycorrhiza inoculant at planting establishes fungal networks from day one, dramatically improving drought resilience, nutrient uptake and establishment success without synthetic inputs.
  • -- Finishing mulch seals moisture into the soil profile, reduces surface temperature, suppresses weed competition, and begins building organic matter as it breaks down.
  • -- Drip irrigation delivers water directly to the root zone, reducing evaporation and ensuring consistent moisture during the critical first establishment period.
  • -- Hand watering in collapses air pockets around roots, initiates soil contact, and gives each plant the best possible start in its new microclimate.

Next steps

  • -- Monitor establishment over the first 4-6 weeks, particularly through autumn into cooler conditions.
  • -- Fauna monitoring to commence -- skink presence confirmed on site from Field Note #001.
  • -- Score reassessment at 6 months once structural layers and canopy begin to establish.
  • -- Species list to be updated to reflect installed count once confirmed on site.

Handmade Habitats

Habitat elements sourced from Handmade Habitats, a local craft producer creating structures from hollowed logs, sticks, dried grasses, native seeds and natural botanical materials. Each piece is set half into the ground -- creating a bridge between the soil layer and the above-ground environment, and providing nesting opportunity, insect refuge and lizard shelter from day one. The cavities and packed natural fibres attract native bees, beetles and small fauna, while the embedded base creates a moist, stable microclimate below. Dried eucalyptus leaves and paper daisies are woven through the structure -- the daisies closed in the rain on installation day, a quiet signal that the garden is already responding to its environment.
