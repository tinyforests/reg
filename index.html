What it is

A public garden profile — with real ecological truth underneath.

This registry isn’t a gallery of pretty gardens (though it will be beautiful). It’s a recording system: a baseline snapshot + ongoing improvements.

  • Designed to be simple, not scientific theatre
  • Place-based: aligned to local ecology and context
  • Built for long timelines: gardens take years

Each registered garden can show what matters: structure, habitat, water, species, and the story of care — without exposing private information.

Public page A shareable profile: photos, story, broad signals.

How it works

Register → baseline → improve → (optionally) earn.

1) Register

Create a profile and choose privacy settings. Add a few photos and basic context.

2) Baseline

Record what’s there: structure, canopy, habitat features, water logic.

3) Improve

Small actions compound. Track upgrades and see the garden lift over time.

For Gardener & Son, this becomes a map of real, functioning gardens — a living index of inner-urban ecological restoration already underway.

Signals

Simple signals. Honest baselines. Visible improvement.

Measurement stays practical — the goal is not bureaucracy, it’s direction. A garden should know where it is, and what “better” means.

  • Canopy / midstorey / groundlayer structure
  • Planting composition (native/indigenous emphasis where relevant)
  • Habitat features (logs, rocks, water, nesting opportunities)
  • Soil cover & permeability (mulch, leaf litter, compacted zones)
  • Water logic (runoff, infiltration, passive irrigation patterns)

Future value

When gardens can be trusted, they can be supported.

The registry is the foundation: it creates the record that incentives can later sit on top of. This is where “making money from a garden” becomes coherent — because outcomes are trackable.

  • Brands can sponsor upgrades with transparent reporting
  • Councils can partner on measurable inner-urban biodiversity lifts
  • Homeowners can receive benefits for real improvements (Yield)
